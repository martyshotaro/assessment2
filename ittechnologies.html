Cloud is referred as a metaphor for “the Internet”, which means a type of computing that is Internet-based computing where different services such as applications, storage, and servers are delivered to a particular organisation’s devices and computers via the Internet. Examples of cloud include Microsoft 365, Salesforce, and Adobe Creative Cloud. Cloud helps in distributing resources, which is often labelled as “federated storage clouds” that leads to being tolerant with faults because of data distribution. Through the use of cloud, it lessens the creation of the files’ different versions because of the shared access to files, documents and data.

For services regarding about cloud computing, there are three main categories, which are IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS.

IaaS stands for “infrastructure as a service”, which refers to the infrastructure resources that are cloud-based that is delivered to organisations through the use of virtualization technology that aids the organisations to manage and build their servers, operating systems, data storage and network. Examples of IaaS include Microsoft Azure and Amazon Web Services (AWS). Also, IaaS helps companies in regards to managing and building their own data as they expand, paying for server and storage space for the foundation of the software and hardware with no hosting and managing servers by themselves on-site. Lastly, the IaaS’ products make up for the structure of building new technologies via cloud.

PaaS stands for “platform as a service”, which refers to the cloud-based platform services that gives the developers a scheme that they can apply to make custom applications. Examples of PaaS include OpenShift and Google App Engine. Lastly, PaaS helps developers construct custom applications online without dealing with storage, data services, and management.

SaaS stands for “software as a service”, which refers to a software that is cloud-based, which is hosted online by a company and it’s available for acquirement on a subscription basis and delivered through the use of internet. Examples of SaaS include Dropbox and JIRA. Also, SaaS helps distributed global teams of individuals who don’t work in terms of close physical concurrence. Also, it helps businesses to manage and use SaaS, which is also highly adaptable and wouldn’t be required to be downloaded and installed via devices to arrange it to a company or a team.

Cloud severs refers to a server that runs in a cloud computing environment. It’s hosted, built, and delivered through the use of cloud computing platform from the internet, which can be accessible remotely. Examples of cloud servers include Amazon elastic Compute Cloud, Google Compute Engine, Azure, HP and Microsoft Gold partnerships and Dell Premier Partner. Cloud servers help in running and functioning as independent units. Also, they help in giving users the security and stability due to the software problems are isolated from your environment. They also avoid hardware issues, which are seen with physical servers that result in being likely to be the most stable option for a business in terms of saving IT costs. Lastly, they offer scalability, which makes it quick and easier to upgrade by adding the memory and disk space.

An autonomous vehicle refers to a vehicle that has the capability to operate itself by performing necessary functions with no human intervention needed through the use of detecting its surroundings. Examples of autonomous vehicles include Autonomous Electric Cars, Autonomous Self-Driving Cars, Race-Ready Autonomous Autos, Self-Parking Car Systems and Traffic Jam-Maneuvering Systems. Autonomous vehicles help in providing advantages that can be compared to human-driven vehicles by increasing the safety on the road, as automated vehicles can potentially omit the amount of casualties as the software that are used in them are likely to commit fewer errors, contrary to humans. Because of this, it can result to a decrease in terms of the number of casualties and accidents and can also reduce traffic congestion. Also, autonomous vehicles can help individuals who aren’t capable to drive due to factors such as disabilities, age or even fatigue in overnight journeys, as a convenient transport system.

The potential impact of autonomous vehicles consists of economic shifts, industrial changes, changes in car insurance and driving safety, and saving time for the commuters. Research projects that the adoption of autonomous vehicles in terms of consumer life and business will have a definite impact/change on the economy.

A study by BCG, a research group predicts that in 2035, 12 million autonomous vehicles and 18 million partially autonomous vehicles will be sold globally each year. Also, autonomous vehicles will hold 25% of the new car market, with market growth somewhere between the $42 billion and $77 billion range. Public transit drivers or taxis are jobs that are currently applied with human intervention can potentially be the outcome of autonomous vehicles operation on their own. Also, manufacturing, construction workers, and farmers could be replaced by autonomous vehicles conceivably, which are projected to do repetitive and specific tasks in those fields.

The farming and mining industries have already adopted autonomous vehicle due to the technology becoming more affordable for them. The use of such vehicles by mining companies aids its worker safety in terms of high-risk environment through the use of taking operators out of their sections when they can be trapped in collapsing tunnels or caves. Also, the use of autonomous vehicles can save on labour costs; reduce carbon dioxide emissions as much as 60% from optimised driving. For construction, the use of loaders, forklifts and excavators can also save on labour costs and reduce carbon dioxide emissions.

Nowadays, consumers will be influenced in terms of how insurance companies manage and conduct their business, as insurance companies might reconsider the traditional business models. Experts have considered a way to ensuring car manufacturers against liability that leads from technical failures, that is linked with autonomous vehicles. Mercedes, Volvo, and Google are the companies that have started self-insuring the products they create.

A goal that was set for driverless cars is to imply a significant health and safety benefit, and they’re expected to reduce the accidents and casualties in the road. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration stated that around 35,092 individuals were killed in 2015 in automobile accidents on U.S. roads and car crashes have a considerable impact on the U.S. economy, leading to costing around $871 billion annually. Also, a recent study from McKinsey & Company, autonomous vehicles and advanced driver assistance systems have the potential to take away the motor vehicle deaths by 90%, that saves thousands of individual’s lives and roughly $190 billion annually in terms of health care figures.

Autonomous vehicles are predicted to save time for the commuters by increasing the flow of the traffic that can lead to the travel times being reduced. Based on the U.S. Census Bureau, Americans spent a total of 29.6 billion hours commuting back in 2014, but with the help of driverless vehicles, a portion of this commute time can be used for reading, accessing, working, relaxing, entertainment and having a better life. However, there are decisions and challenges that arise before autonomous vehicles can take over that include the impact of consumers and businesses, the increased safety for the passengers and drivers, the reduced health care and the labour costs for businesses.
